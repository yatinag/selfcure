Coffee

If you decide to reduce or eliminate coffee, you may experience cravings. These cravings come from both your psychological and physical dependence on the drug. Not only is drinking coffee a pleasurable daily habit, it becomes a physiological dependence as well. If you manage to resist the cravings, they will subside after a few days. Overall, it's easier to break the coffee habit than many other addictions, and there are many good reasons for doing so.

"Resisting your urges", is easier said than done, however decreasing your consumption gradually over a period of days (or weeks, if you're a heavy consumer) to avoid being plagued by withdrawal symptoms, is a recognised way by all scientists to make your recover much easier.

Also you must know when you have had too much, and set levels for caffeine consumption rather than coffee consumption. About 300-400mg of Caffeine per day are considered as a safety limit for an adult and should not be exceeded in order for him or her to remain healthy. Coffee addicts usually average to about 600-700mgs of Caffeine per day which is much higher the safety limits. You can try decaf or coffee with low fat milk to bring down your daily caffeeine consumptions.

Most importantly, remember to reward yourself after any bit of success that you have on your path to recovery, after all you deserve it!!
