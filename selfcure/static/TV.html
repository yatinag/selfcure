TV Shows

You must first understand the true purpose behind you watching a TV show. Do you actually learn anything from it? Even if you do, is the received information worth all that time that you have spent on it. In most cases, you already know that it is wrong, but you obviously need a strong will power, but hold on.

You must try to identify your Trigger. For example, some people have a urge to watch TV right after dinner. So, if your trigger is "watch TV after dinner", you can try to replace it with a trigger called - "Go for a walk after dinner". Also you should identify your experience after you watch TV. Does it give you the kind of pleasure you can also get from anywhere else? Try to find out what else can you do in order to get the same pleasure by doing something else. For example, reading a book or working out.

Lastly, a good way to think about this is to ask yourself "What am I missing in my life". Are you missing company of a loved one, are you missing the joy of open air or endless moments of fun with friends. Unless you really "start" missing something badly, you won't get a strong desire to change this habit.

Most importantly, remember to reward yourself after any bit of success that you have on your path to recovery, after all you deserve it!!
