Nerdy Talk

There are some anti-obesity medications (Sibutramine (Reductil or Meridia)) or appetite suppressants that work on the brain and may help to reduce symptoms of food addiction. They also tend to have side effects such as they may increase blood pressure, cause dry mouth, constipation, headache, and insomnia. One may try to talk to their doctor about their food addiction, however, unfortunately, very few health professionals even know that food addiction exists, let alone how to treat it.

Setting Boundaries With Unsafe Foods

Typically, trigger or “unsafe” foods should be removed from the diet and boundaries should set so that managing these foods in a healthier way can be relearned. If someone binges on ice cream when he or she is stressed, it’s best not to keep it in the house. Eliminating the temptation until he or she can eat ice cream again in a balanced way is a safe option.

Surely only health can not be kept in mind, as sometimes one may also eat "junk food", however a moderation is a mandate. You must make a schedule, which lists the items that you wouldn't be having for a particular period of time, that one may list as "unsafe". Preferable go out for a meal, once a week and eat all the junk you can in that one meal only.
